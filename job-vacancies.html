Job role: Admin Officer

We’re looking for an Administrative Officer at the London Community Bank.

This is an amazing opportunity to join a London based Credit Union in an important role supporting their members, carrying out critical operational tasks and offering excellent customer service.

If you are highly organised, have lots of initiative and love improving processes and systems and keen on learning about a new sector, this could be just the role for you. The role offers flexible working, a collaborative team environment and room for growth.

About Us:

The London Community Bank is a thriving credit union with a membership base of 3500 and savings of £8m. We're committed to financial inclusion and community development. We were formed in January 1991 originally just serving the employees of Hillingdon Council. Since then, our Common Bond has extended to anyone who works or lives in the London boroughs of Hillingdon, Harrow, Ealing, Brent, Hounslow, Hammersmith & Fulham, Richmond upon Thames, Kingston upon Thames, Wandsworth, Merton as well as Slough and South Bucks. We are proud of the impact we have had on our local communities are growing our team to enable to us to continue to serve our existing members and grow our member base. We are lean team that work collaboratively and support each other’s learning and growth. We focus on outcomes, performance and impact to the members and communities we serve.

About You:

Are you a self-motivated, organized individual with practical office and financial management experience? We're looking for a confident communicator, team-player and someone that is eager to learn, to join our team, managing day-to-day activities and promoting financial services.

Position Details:

Job Title: Administration Officer

Reporting to: General Manager

Salary: £24,300 p.a. pro rata – up to 25 hours per week (9 am to 2 pm Monday to Friday) plus pension contributions

Location: The Civic Centre, Uxbridge

Main Responsibilities:

  • Collaborate with the wider team, supporting each other and contributing to the overall performance of the team.
  • Undertake time-critical payments for all product offerings.
  • Deliver quality services to members. Provide prompt, friendly, and reliable service to members. Ensure responsiveness and accountability to members. Gather member feedback and address issues raised.
  • Learn and operate the Credit Union's accounting system and manual records.
  • To operate within operating policies and procedures.
  • Collaborate with the wider team to implement new product offerings.
  • Act as an advocate of the credit union and promote the products and services.
  • Ensure security, insurance compliance, and staff safety
  • Manage ICT systems, filing, scanning, and banking duties.
  • Ensure legal and regulatory compliance.
  • Demonstrate commitment to equal opportunities and attend relevant training.

 The successful candidate will demonstrate the following:

-Excellent communication, organizational skills and attention to detail.

-Strong customer service skills.

- Keen to learn about / grow their knowledge of the Credit Union sector and regulatory framework. Passionate about financial inclusion and serving the local community.

- Proficiency in database management, following processes, identifying improvements and passion to get the most from a system.

- Ability to work well in a team.

- Tech literate with practical office and financial management experience

- As is usual in a small team, happy to take on whatever is a priority at the time.

- Self-starter with the ability to work under pressure.

If this role sounds interesting and like a good fit for your skills and experience, we would love to hear from you at:

info@Londoncb.co.uk.

If you have questions about the role, you can also reach out to us at the email address above.
